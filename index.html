Welcome.

Please enjoy this gallery of six artworks that I find at least mildly appealing.

Nude Descending Staircase No. 2 (1912)

Marcel Duchamp

Style: Cubism
Media: Oil on Canvas
Current Location: Philadelphia Museum of Art

An art critic may tell you that this painting is fascinating because it shines a clear light on Duchamp's obsession with motion over figure. However, I find this piece to be at least mildly appealing because of its descending composition.

Soir Bleu (1914)

Edward Hopper

Period: Neoclassicism, American Realism
Media: Oil on Canvas
Current Location: Whitney Museum of American Art

Soir Bleu is a very famous American painting, one which displays Hopper's anguish with his career. I find this piece at least slightly amusing due to the composition of light in colour.

Glenn (1984)

Jean-Michel Basquiat

Period: Neo-Expressionism
Media: Acrylic, Oilstick and Xerox on Canvas
Current Location: Private Collection

Basquiat is an incredibly controversial artist, many think his following is based on pretentiousness. However, I find his use of art as a personal therapeutical outburst without defined meaning as something that should be more consistent in the art world when given grounded boundaries.

The Elephants (1948)

Salvador Dali

Style: Surrealism
Media: Oil on Canvas
Location: Private Collection

This is an interesting painting. Salvador "God" Dali is known for his complete adoption of the surrealism manifesto as a lifestyle. The obelisks of domination balance on frail legs.

La Barricade (1949)

Bernard Buffet

Style: Miserabilism, Expressionism
Genre: Symbolic Art
Media: Unknown
Current Location: Confidential

Buffet is amongst the most successful miserabilists and I believe this specific painting highlights the superbity of his composition amongst the expresionists.

Self-Portrait with Physalis (1912)

Egon Schiele

Period: Expressionism, Vienna Secession
Media: Unidentified
Current Location: Leopold Museum

Schiele, the prodigal son of Gustav Klimt. This piece is one of the few that reflects his own confidence in himself, the composition is set and defined, the work more realistic than most of his others, each line controlled, and his gaze matching the observer.


I hope you enjoyed your time viewing this gallery of artworks I find at least mildly appealing.

My apologies to George Rowlett, who only made it to an honorable mention, despite being more proficient than Duchamp.
